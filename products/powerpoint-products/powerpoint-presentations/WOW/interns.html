About This Product

Interns & Internships (Comprehensive) PowerPoint Presentation 137 slides with Participant Handout

Internships give college students and recent graduates the chance to gain experience while providing businesses with low-cost labor and the chance to train potential full-time employees. A successful internship program benefits both the interns and the employers, and provides real value to growing businesses. But where do you start, and how?

Learning Objectives:

  • Learn the value of hiring interns for your business or organization
  • Understand the necessary differences between paid and unpaid interns, and various modes of compensation
  • Learn how to set up your internship opportunity and get the word out for recruitment
  • Be able to narrow down internship applicants to the most valuable candidates
  • Pick up useful techniques for getting the most out of your teams
  • Learn how to conduct evaluations and use those evaluations to streamline your internship program

PowerPoint Presentation Content Slides Include:

  • Definition/s of an intern/internship
  • Learning objectives of this presentation
  • Etymology of interns and internships
  • Overview of the legality of taking on interns
  • 10 slides of Various ways to compensate interns
  • 26 slides of How to recruit interns
    • 15 points of Recruitment methods
  • 23 slides of Screening and selection of interns
    • 8 points of What to(and what not) to look for in candidates
    • 13 points of Interviewing candidates
    • 6 points of Making the final selection
  • 13 slides of How to get the most out of your interns
    • 11 points of Do’s and don’ts
    • 14 points of What to discuss with new interns
  • 11 slides of Screening and selecting volunteers
    • 3 slides of Building an application
    • 5 slides of Conducting interviews
  • 16 slides of Supervision and evaluation
    • 11 points of Supervision and work styles
    • 10 points of Evaluating interns
    • 12 points of Evaluating your program
  • 4 slides of Offering jobs to interns
  • 9 slides of Improving your program
  • 16 action steps and much more

Also included are: 3 diagrams/charts, & 11 high resolution photographs.
