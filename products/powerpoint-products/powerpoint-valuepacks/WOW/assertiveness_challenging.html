Assertiveness 155 slides with Participant Handout

This Assertiveness PowerPoint Presentation allows individuals to become aware of their aggressive, passive, and positive behaviors, and develop the confidence to be assertive in today's workplace.

Learning Objectives:

  • Understand the philosophy of being assertive
  • Recognize the differences between being assertive and being aggressive
  • Understand these differences through the exploration of personal reactions to given situations
  • Participate in role-playing exercises to practice the tenets of being assertive
  • Gain the courage to be assertive in the most appropriate and effective way
  • Generate an action plan to enhance your assertive communication skills

Challenging Employees 149 slides with Participant Handout

Every workplace can be filled with different and unique personalities. Knowing how to react to and handle these, sometimes challenging, employees is a critical skill to develop.

Did you know that... the word "Challenging" (n.) c. 1200, "to rebuke," from Old French chalongier "complain, protest; haggle, quibble," from Vulgar Latin *calumniare "to accuse falsely," Latin calumniari "to accuse falsely, misrepresent, slander," from calumnia "trickery". (MORE) Late 13c. as "to object to, take exception to;" c. 1300 as "to accuse," especially "to accuse falsely," also "to call to account;" late 14c. as "to call to fight."

Learning Objectives:

  • Be able to locate and identify difficult employees
  • Learn that every employee has a unique way of completing tasks, and learn how to gel everyone's abilities
  • Learn how to deal with a continually troubling employee
  • Understand what is expected and how to work more effectively in the office environment
  • Learn the tools that allow for constructive feedback and more productive employees
